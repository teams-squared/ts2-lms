# Data & Communications

This module covers how to handle client and company data correctly — what tools are approved for sharing files, what is strictly off-limits, and how to behave responsibly in everyday communication and collaboration.

## Approved Transfer Methods

Teams Squared is a **cloud-first organisation**. All work data must be stored and shared through approved systems only.

**Approved platforms:**

- Microsoft 365 (Outlook, Teams, SharePoint, OneDrive) — within the Teams Squared tenant or a client's tenant
- Client-assigned systems (e.g. client document management tools, ticketing platforms, or cloud environments), where explicitly authorised

**The key rule:** No work products, attachments, screenshots, or client data may be shared outside these approved systems without written approval.

## Prohibited Transfer Methods

The following are **strictly prohibited** for any work-related data transfer:

- WhatsApp, Telegram, Signal, or SMS
- Personal email accounts
- Social media platforms
- Unapproved instant messaging tools
- Personal cloud storage (Google Drive, Dropbox, iCloud, etc.)

If you genuinely need to use a non-standard method, you must get written approval from the Cybersecurity Team in advance. The transfer must be time-bound, purpose-limited, and documented.

## Client Data Segregation

Data belonging to different clients must be kept entirely separate. Always store client data within the repository or system designated for that specific client.

Do not move, copy, or store client data in shared, unassigned, or unapproved locations.

## Keeping Copies to a Minimum

Client and company data should not be duplicated unnecessarily. Where possible, share a secure link to the original source rather than creating copies of files.

Only maintain the minimum number of local copies required to do the work. Local files must be reviewed weekly and deleted as soon as they are no longer needed.

## Sharing Links

When sharing documents or files via a link:

- Configure appropriate access controls before sending (limit to the minimum number of authorised people)
- Apply link expiry dates and download restrictions where the platform supports it
- Never use public links or guest access without explicit approval from Teams Squared and the relevant client
- Review all active shared links within **one week** of sharing and remove them as soon as they are no longer needed

## Screen Sharing & Video Calls

Before sharing your screen in any meeting, call, or video session:

1. Close all unrelated documents, tabs, and applications
2. Hide or close any notifications that might reveal sensitive information
3. Ensure no other client's data is visible

You are responsible for ensuring confidential information is only visible to the authorised participants in the session.

## Printing

The printing of client or company data is **prohibited** unless you have prior written approval from Teams Squared or the relevant client.

If printing is approved:

- Store printed materials securely
- Never leave them unattended or visible to unauthorised persons
- Destroy them securely (shred) when they are no longer needed

This applies regardless of where you are working — including at home.

## Using AI Tools

Client or confidential information must **not** be entered into, uploaded to, or processed by any AI tool, automated processing service, or third-party platform unless explicitly approved by both Teams Squared and the relevant client.

This includes tools like ChatGPT, Copilot (outside the approved Microsoft 365 tenant), Gemini, and similar services. When in doubt, ask the Cybersecurity Team before using any external tool with client data.

## Internet & Website Use

Your company device's internet access must be used for legitimate work purposes only. The following are prohibited:

- Pornographic or sexually explicit content
- Weapons, explosives, or violent material
- Gambling or illegal substances
- Hate speech, extremist content, or unlawful activities
- Pirated software, cracked tools, or circumvention utilities
- Illegal, malicious, or harmful websites of any kind

Bitdefender is deployed on your device to block known malicious websites, but no technical control is perfect. Use your judgement, and if something looks suspicious, don't click it — report it.

## Phishing & Suspicious Links

Never click on suspicious links, open unexpected attachments, or respond to unsolicited requests for credentials or sensitive information.

If you receive something suspicious — an unexpected email asking you to log in somewhere, an attachment you weren't expecting, or a message that feels off — report it to the Cybersecurity Team immediately.

> **Example subject line:** `Phishing – Suspicious email from external sender`

## Accidental Data Disclosure

If you accidentally send client or company data to the wrong person:

1. **Do not wait** — notify the Cybersecurity Team immediately
2. Take steps to revoke access or delete the shared link if possible
3. Treat it as a security incident — it will be handled through the Incident Management process

The sooner you report it, the more we can do to contain the impact. You will not be penalised for reporting an honest mistake promptly.
