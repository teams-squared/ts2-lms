# Passwords & Access

This module covers the rules for creating and managing passwords, setting up your password manager and MFA, and how access to systems is granted and removed at Teams Squared.

## Password Requirements

All passwords used on Teams Squared systems must meet the following minimum standards:

- At least **12 characters** long
- Contains at least one **uppercase letter**
- Contains at least one **lowercase letter**
- Contains at least one **number**
- Contains at least one **special character** (e.g. `! @ # $ %`)
- Is **not reused** across different systems or accounts
- Does **not contain personal information** (name, email address, date of birth, etc.)
- Does **not appear** in any known breach database (we use [HaveIBeenPwned](https://haveibeenpwned.com) as a reference)

> **Never share your password with anyone** — not your manager, not a colleague, not anyone claiming to be from the IT or Cybersecurity Team. We will never ask for your password.

If you believe a password has been compromised, change it immediately and notify the Cybersecurity Team.

## 1Password — Your Password Manager

Teams Squared uses **1Password** as its company-wide password manager. You will receive an email invitation to set up your 1Password account during onboarding.

**All credentials must be stored in 1Password.** This means you must not store passwords in:

- Your browser's built-in password manager
- Notes apps, spreadsheets, or documents
- Personal devices or personal password managers

### Setting Up 1Password

1. Check your onboarding email for your 1Password invitation
2. Create your account and set a strong master password (it must meet the password requirements above)
3. Enable MFA on your 1Password account (see below)
4. Save your emergency recovery codes somewhere secure and offline
5. Install the 1Password browser extension and desktop app on your company device

> **Important:** Do not use any alternative password manager. If you have a specific need, raise it with the Cybersecurity Team in advance and get written approval.

## Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)

MFA is **mandatory** on all systems that support it. This includes:

- Your email account
- 1Password
- All cloud platforms
- Any administrative or privileged accounts

### Setting Up MFA

During onboarding you will receive an email specifying the recommended authenticator apps to install. Accepted MFA methods include:

- Authenticator apps (e.g. Microsoft Authenticator, Google Authenticator)
- Hardware security keys
- One-time codes

Work through the following setup steps as part of your onboarding:

1. Install the recommended authenticator app on your mobile device
2. Enable MFA on your Microsoft 365 account
3. Enable MFA on your 1Password account
4. Enable MFA on any other company-assigned platforms as instructed

> **Tip:** Do this on Day 1. Access to systems without MFA may be restricted or blocked entirely.

## How Access Is Granted

Access to Teams Squared systems and client environments is granted only after:

- Your engagement or employment has been formally approved
- Required agreements (NDA, contract) are in place
- Onboarding checks are completed

Access is aligned to your assigned role and client — you will only be given what you need to do your job. This is called the **least privilege** principle.

You will never be given access to a client's systems simply because you work at Teams Squared. Access to each client is granted separately, scoped to your assigned work.

## Access Reviews & Changes

If your role or client assignment changes, your access will be reviewed and adjusted. You should not retain access to systems or client environments beyond what your current assignment requires.

If you believe you have access that you no longer need, notify the Cybersecurity Team so it can be removed promptly.

## When You Leave or Move On

When your engagement ends, **all access is revoked immediately**. This includes:

- System and platform accounts
- VPN access
- Company device (which will be remotely locked and wiped)

You must stop using all Teams Squared systems and devices and comply with all offboarding instructions as soon as you are instructed to do so.

## Sharing Credentials

In general, **credentials must never be shared**. If you are ever in a situation where sharing a credential is genuinely unavoidable, you must:

1. Get prior written approval from the Cybersecurity Team
2. Use only approved sharing methods: **Password Pusher** or **1Password**
3. Set a maximum of **5 access attempts** and a **24-hour expiry** on any shared link

Never share credentials via email, Teams chat, WhatsApp, or any other messaging tool.

## Privileged & Admin Accounts

If you hold a privileged or administrative account:

- It must use credentials entirely separate from your standard user account
- It must have MFA enabled
- It must be stored in 1Password
- It must not be used for routine day-to-day tasks
- At least 2 and no more than 3 authorised persons must know the password

Changes immediately if compromise is suspected.
