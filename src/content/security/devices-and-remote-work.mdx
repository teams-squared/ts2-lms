# Devices & Remote Work

Teams Squared is a remote-first company. This module covers how to use your company device correctly, how to connect securely, and how to work safely whether you're at home, in a café, or travelling.

## Your Company Device

You will be issued a Teams Squared-managed Windows laptop before you start work. This device has been provisioned by the Cybersecurity Team with a hardened baseline configuration — security settings, encryption, endpoint protection, and access controls are all pre-configured.

**Only this device may be used for work.** Personal laptops, tablets, or phones (BYOD) are not permitted unless you have explicit written approval from the Cybersecurity Team.

### What You Must Not Do With Your Device

- Share it with anyone — family members, friends, or any other unauthorised person
- Allow anyone else to log in, use, or connect peripherals to it
- Install software, browser extensions, or tools without prior written approval from the Cybersecurity Team
- Modify system security settings or disable any security controls
- Remove the asset identification markings
- Create local unencrypted backups of company or client data
- Connect USB drives, external storage, or unauthorised peripheral devices (USB storage is blocked by default)

If you need a software tool that isn't already on your device, contact the Cybersecurity Team for approval before installing anything.

### Encryption

Your device has full disk encryption enabled. This was configured before the device was issued to you and must not be disabled. Encryption recovery keys are held centrally by the Cybersecurity Team.

### Keeping Your Device Updated

Security patches and system updates are managed centrally. When an update is pushed to your device, do not dismiss or defer it. Keep your device connected to the internet regularly so updates can be applied.

> **Critical patches are required to be deployed within 7 days of release.** Do not block or delay this process.

## The Company VPN

All remote access to Teams Squared or client systems must go through the **company VPN**. You will be given VPN access during onboarding.

### When You Must Use the VPN

The VPN must be enabled **before** accessing:

- Any Teams Squared systems or tools
- Any client systems or client data

This applies everywhere — including your home network. There are no locations where VPN use is optional, unless you have an explicit written exception from the Cybersecurity Team.

> **Working from a café, airport, or hotel Wi-Fi without the VPN is a serious policy breach and may result in contract termination.**

### VPN Rules

- Never disable or bypass the VPN without authorisation
- Never use a personal or unapproved VPN service
- If a client requires you to use their own VPN, notify the Cybersecurity Team first and follow the approved instructions

If you need to temporarily disable the VPN for a technical reason, get written approval from the Cybersecurity Team before accessing any systems.

## Declaring Your Work Location

You must declare a **primary remote working location** to Teams Squared. This is the location you work from most of the time.

If your primary location changes for a sustained period, notify Teams Squared. Prior written approval is required if the change:

- Involves a different country or jurisdiction
- Involves regular use of a public or shared environment
- Introduces additional security or connectivity risks

Short-term travel does not require approval, provided you follow the VPN rules and this policy throughout.

If you expect to work away from your primary location for more than **20 hours per week**, get written approval in advance.

## Working in Public & Shared Environments

When working from a café, co-working space, hotel, airport, or similar:

- **Lock your screen** whenever you step away, even briefly
- **Position your screen** so others cannot easily see it (sit with your back to a wall where possible)
- **Use a headset** for any call involving client or sensitive information
- **Never leave your device unattended** — not even for a moment
- **Close confidential documents** and unrelated tabs before any screen-sharing session
- **Avoid discussing sensitive client information** where conversations could be overheard

You must not show documents, files, or work products to members of the public under any circumstances.

## Travelling Internationally

If you need to take your device outside the country:

1. Notify the Cybersecurity Team **in advance** so they can address any compliance or security considerations
2. Maintain physical control of your device at all times while travelling
3. Report any loss, theft, or forced access immediately

> If your device is ever subject to an unusual inspection, seizure, or forced access attempt at a border or checkpoint, report it to the Cybersecurity Team as soon as possible — even if nothing appeared to be compromised.

## Social Engineering

Be alert to attempts by unknown individuals to:

- Ask to view your screen or documents
- Request credentials, access, or "quick help" with a system
- Distract you while you are using your device

Any suspicious interaction should be treated as a potential security incident and reported to the Cybersecurity Team.

## Locking & Screen Security

- Your device must be locked **every time you step away**, no matter how briefly
- Automatic screen lock is configured on your device and must not be disabled
- Never leave your screen visible and unattended

## Lost, Stolen, or Compromised Devices

Report any lost, stolen, or compromised device to the Cybersecurity Team **immediately**. Teams Squared can remotely lock and wipe company devices — the sooner you report, the sooner we can act to protect client data.
