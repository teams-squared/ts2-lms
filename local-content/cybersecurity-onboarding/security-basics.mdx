# Security Basics

Welcome to Teams Squared's security onboarding. This document covers the essential security principles you need to follow from day one.

## Why Security Matters

Every team member plays a role in keeping our systems and data safe. A single compromised account or misconfigured setting can expose customer data and damage trust built over years.

## Password Hygiene

- Use a password manager (1Password is provided to all staff)
- Never reuse passwords across services
- All accounts must use a password of at least 16 characters
- Enable MFA on every account that supports it — this is mandatory for all company systems

## Phishing Awareness

- Never click links in unexpected emails, even from known senders
- When in doubt, verify the sender by calling or messaging them directly
- Report suspicious emails to `security@teamssquared.com` immediately
- IT will never ask for your password via email or chat

## Device Security

- Lock your screen whenever you step away (`Cmd+Ctrl+Q` on Mac, `Win+L` on Windows)
- Enable full-disk encryption (FileVault on Mac, BitLocker on Windows)
- Never connect to public Wi-Fi without the company VPN active
- Keep your OS and software up to date — enable automatic updates

## What to Do If Something Goes Wrong

If you suspect a security incident — compromised credentials, suspicious login, data exposure — report it immediately:

1. Do **not** try to investigate or fix it yourself
2. Contact the security team at `security@teamssquared.com` or Slack `#security-incidents`
3. Preserve evidence: don't delete emails, logs, or files
4. Document what you observed and when
