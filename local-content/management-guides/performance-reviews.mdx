# Running Performance Reviews

Performance reviews at Teams Squared happen twice a year: **June** (mid-year) and **December** (year-end). This guide covers the manager's role in the process.

## Timeline

| Date | Action |
|------|--------|
| 3 weeks before | Self-assessment forms open in the People portal |
| 2 weeks before | Peer feedback collection closes |
| 1 week before | Manager review written and submitted |
| Review week | 1-on-1 review meetings held |
| 1 week after | Outcomes recorded; compensation changes submitted |

## Writing the review

A strong review is:

- **Specific** — reference actual events, not generalisations
- **Balanced** — acknowledge strengths and growth areas equally
- **Forward-looking** — include clear goals for the next period
- **Fair** — calibrate against the levelling rubric, not your personal expectations

### Structure

```
1. Summary (2–3 sentences)
2. Key accomplishments (3–5 bullet points)
3. Areas for development (2–3 areas)
4. Goals for next period (SMART format)
5. Rating: Exceeds / Meets / Needs Improvement
```

## Rating calibration

All manager ratings go through a calibration session with your skip-level before being shared with employees. This ensures consistency across teams. Avoid inflating or deflating ratings before calibration — adjustments will be made there.

## Delivering difficult feedback

If you're delivering a "Needs Improvement" rating:

1. Don't surprise them — they should know the situation from prior 1-on-1s
2. Be direct, kind, and specific: *"Your delivery on the Q1 roadmap was 2 weeks late on 3 of 5 projects"*, not *"your delivery needs work"*
3. Pair every criticism with a concrete suggestion
4. Document the conversation in the People portal

## Compensation changes

After calibration, any salary adjustments are submitted by your VP to Finance. You are not authorised to make or imply compensation commitments outside this process.
